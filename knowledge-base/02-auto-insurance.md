# Auto Insurance — Coverage Details

## What is typically included

Coverage availability depends on state law and the plan you select.

- **Bodily injury liability** — Pays others’ injury claims when you are legally at fault, up to policy limits.
- **Property damage liability** — Pays for damage you cause to others’ property (e.g., another vehicle or structure).
- **Collision** — Pays for damage to your insured vehicle from a collision with another object or vehicle, minus your deductible.
- **Comprehensive** — Pays for damage from non-collision events such as theft, fire, vandalism, hail, or animal strikes, minus your deductible.
- **Uninsured/underinsured motorist** — Where offered, may cover your injuries or damage when an at-fault driver has no or insufficient insurance.
- **Medical payments / PIP** — Where required or optional, may cover medical expenses for you and passengers regardless of fault (varies by state).

## Common exclusions

The following are **generally not covered** unless your policy explicitly says otherwise:

- Intentional damage or illegal use of the vehicle
- Racing or competitive events
- Wear and tear, mechanical breakdown, or maintenance
- Using the vehicle for paid rideshare or delivery without the proper endorsement
- Damage while the driver is unlicensed, impaired, or excluded on the policy
- Personal belongings inside the car (often covered under home/renters, not auto)

Always read your declarations page and endorsements for your exact contract.
