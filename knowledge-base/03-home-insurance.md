# Home Insurance — Coverage Details

“Home” here means **homeowners**, **condo**, or **renters** policies where ShieldBase offers them. Wording differs by form; below is a typical structure.

## What is typically included

- **Dwelling (HO-3 / similar)** — Physical structure of the home against covered perils (fire, wind, hail, etc., per policy).
- **Other structures** — Detached garage, fence, shed, within a percentage of dwelling limit unless scheduled.
- **Personal property** — Furniture, clothing, electronics, subject to limits for jewelry, cash, collectibles unless scheduled.
- **Loss of use** — Extra living costs if a covered loss makes the home uninhabitable, up to limits and time caps.
- **Personal liability** — Legal defense and damages if you are liable for bodily injury or property damage to others.
- **Medical payments to others** — Small amounts for guest injuries on your property, regardless of fault (not health insurance).

**Renters** policies usually cover personal property and liability, not the building (that is the landlord’s coverage).

## Common exclusions

- Flood and earthquake (often require separate policies or endorsements)
- Maintenance, mold from long-term neglect, or gradual seepage
- Business operations beyond incidental limits
- Intentional acts
- War, nuclear hazard, and certain government actions
- High-value items above sub-limits unless listed (e.g., fine art, firearms)

Review your policy’s **named perils vs. open perils** section for the dwelling and **special limits** for personal property.
