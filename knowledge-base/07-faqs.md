# Frequently Asked Questions

## Eligibility

**Who can buy a policy?**  
Eligible adults with an insurable interest and acceptable underwriting/rating factors. Auto and home require a valid address in a state where ShieldBase is authorized. Life requires insurable interest and passing underwriting.

**Can I insure a vehicle I don’t own?**  
Usually only with a documented insurable interest (e.g., co-owner, lessor arrangement). Rules vary; ask before binding.

## Cancellation

**Can I cancel anytime?**  
Yes, subject to state rules. You may cancel mid-term; earned premium and **short-rate** or **pro-rata** refunds depend on state and policy terms. We may cancel or non-renew only for permitted reasons defined by law and your contract, with required notice.

**Non-renewal**  
We may choose not to renew at renewal for underwriting or eligibility reasons, with advance notice as required.

## Bundling and discounts

**Multi-policy discount**  
Bundling **auto + home** (or **auto + renters**) on one account often reduces both lines by a **combined illustrative 8–15%** compared to standalone pricing in comparable scenarios—actual discount varies.

**Other common discounts (where available)**  
Safe driver, multi-vehicle, paid-in-full, paperless, protective devices (home), new home, good student (auto).

## Billing

**Payment plans**  
Monthly EFT/card or pay-in-full. Late payments may incur fees and, after notice, risk of cancellation for non-payment.

**Can I change my payment date?**  
In many cases yes, within available billing windows. Date-change options may be limited when an account is past due or close to renewal.

## Policy changes

**How do I add or remove a driver?**  
You can request changes through policy service channels. Driver additions may require license details and can change premium immediately or at next term, depending on effective date rules.

**How do I update my deductible or limits?**  
Most limit and deductible changes can be requested mid-term. Premium impact depends on product line, state filings, and any underwriting restrictions.

**Can I backdate a policy change?**  
Generally no, except where required by law or supported by documented processing error.

## Claims follow-up

**How do I check claim status?**  
Use the claim number in the app/portal or contact claims support. Status typically moves from reported, to inspection/review, to estimate, to payment/settlement.

**Can I choose my own repair shop?**  
Usually yes for auto and home repairs, but preferred networks may provide faster handling or warranty advantages. Payment remains subject to policy terms and approved estimate scope.

**Why was part of my claim not paid?**  
Partial payments can happen due to deductibles, exclusions, limits, depreciation rules, or documentation gaps. Request the written explanation of benefits if any line item is unclear.

## Documents and proof of insurance

**Where can I get ID cards and declarations pages?**  
Digital copies are typically available in the app/portal, with mailed copies available on request where applicable.

**Can I add a mortgagee or lienholder?**  
Yes. Provide the institution name, mailing address, and loan/reference number so records can be updated correctly.
