# Insurance Glossary (Plain Language)

This glossary defines common insurance terms used across ShieldBase support conversations.

## A-D

- **Actual cash value (ACV):** Replacement cost minus depreciation for age/condition.
- **Adjuster:** Professional who investigates claims and evaluates covered loss amounts.
- **Binder:** Temporary evidence of coverage before final policy issuance.
- **Claim:** Request for payment or services after a covered loss event.
- **Coverage limit:** Maximum amount the policy pays for a covered claim category.
- **Declarations page:** Policy summary showing insureds, coverages, limits, deductibles, and premium.
- **Deductible:** Amount policyholder pays before insurer pays the covered remainder.

## E-L

- **Endorsement:** Policy add-on that changes coverage terms, limits, or exclusions.
- **Exclusion:** Specific situation or cause of loss not covered by policy terms.
- **Grace period:** Additional time to pay premium before policy lapses, if provided.
- **Insurable interest:** Financial or personal stake required to insure a person or property.
- **Liability coverage:** Coverage for damages/injuries you are legally responsible to pay.
- **Loss of use:** Extra living expense coverage when home is uninhabitable from a covered loss.

## M-R

- **Medical payments (MedPay):** Limited no-fault medical coverage for injuries, where offered.
- **Named insured:** Person(s) listed with primary policy rights and responsibilities.
- **Peril:** Cause of loss (fire, theft, wind, etc.) that may be covered or excluded.
- **Premium:** Amount paid to keep insurance coverage active.
- **Proof of loss:** Formal signed statement of claim details and amount requested.
- **Replacement cost:** Cost to repair/replace with similar kind and quality without depreciation.
- **Rider:** Life-policy term similar to endorsement; modifies base coverage.

## S-Z

- **Sub-limit:** Lower maximum within a broader coverage section.
- **Subrogation:** Insurer's right to recover paid claim amounts from responsible third parties.
- **Term life:** Life insurance covering a set period without cash value accumulation.
- **Underwriting:** Process used to evaluate risk, eligibility, and premium classification.
- **Uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM):** Coverage that may protect you if at-fault driver lacks sufficient insurance.

## Quick explanation patterns

When a customer asks for a definition, use:

1. One-sentence plain-language definition.
2. One practical example.
3. One reminder that policy wording controls.

Example:  
"A deductible is the amount you pay first on a covered claim. If your covered repair is `$3,000` and your deductible is `$500`, insurance may pay the remaining covered amount after review. Your declarations page confirms the exact deductible."
