# Endorsements and Optional Coverages

ShieldBase offers optional endorsements to tailor baseline coverage. Availability and limits vary by state, underwriting profile, and selected tier.

## Auto optional coverages

### Rental reimbursement

- Helps pay for a temporary rental car when your insured vehicle is in a covered repair.
- Typical daily/total options (illustrative): `$30/$900`, `$40/$1,200`, or `$50/$1,500`.
- Waiting periods and eligible rental classes may apply.

### Roadside assistance

- 24/7 dispatch for covered events such as tow, battery jump, lockout, flat tire, and fuel delivery.
- Service-call cap may apply by policy period (for example, up to 4 covered service calls).
- Non-emergency towing distance limit may apply.

### New car replacement

- Available for newer vehicles meeting age/mileage requirements at policy inception.
- If a covered total loss occurs during the eligibility window, settlement may be based on replacing with a new vehicle of like kind and quality rather than actual cash value.

### Gap-like loan/lease assistance

- For financed/leased vehicles with eligible terms.
- May help pay some or all of the difference between the settlement amount and payoff balance after a covered total loss.
- Does not cover missed payments, late fees, or penalties unrelated to the loss.

### Accident forgiveness (eligibility-based)

- May prevent premium impact from one qualifying at-fault accident after a claim-free period.
- Usually unavailable after severe violations or repeated losses.

## Home optional coverages

### Scheduled personal property

- Adds itemized coverage for jewelry, fine art, musical instruments, camera gear, or collectibles.
- Typically requires appraisals/receipts above threshold values.
- Broader perils and lower/no deductible may apply to scheduled items.

### Water backup and sump overflow

- Covers direct physical loss from sewer/drain backup or sump overflow up to selected sub-limit.
- Often excluded from base forms unless endorsed.

### Equipment breakdown

- Covers sudden accidental breakdown of covered home systems and appliances (for example, HVAC, water heater, certain smart systems).
- Not a substitute for routine maintenance.

### Home cyber support (where offered)

- May provide limited reimbursement and response support for events such as online fraud, cyber extortion support services, and data recovery.
- Service scope and payout caps are defined by endorsement wording.

### Identity restoration

- Provides case-management support and limited expense reimbursement for covered identity theft events.
- Can include document replacement, notary fees, and lost wages for remediation time (subject to caps).

## Life optional riders

### Child term rider

- Provides a small benefit on covered dependent children while rider is active.
- Convertible options may be available at majority age depending on rider terms.

### Waiver of premium (disability)

- Waives eligible premiums after a waiting period if insured meets rider disability definition.
- Proof requirements and periodic review apply.

### Accidental death benefit

- Pays an additional amount for covered accidental death events, as defined.
- Exclusions and activity restrictions apply.

## Packaging guidance for conversational support

When discussing options with customers, agents typically:

1. Confirm baseline needs (state minimum vs preferred limits).
2. Ask about risk tolerance and replacement budget.
3. Compare deductible trade-offs with endorsement value.
4. Recommend documenting high-value assets before policy issuance.
